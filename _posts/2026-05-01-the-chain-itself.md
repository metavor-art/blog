A blog is not a collection of posts. It is one post that keeps starting.

Each entry arrives with a date and a title, and we treat these as if they are independent objects — discrete essays with their own boundaries. But they are not. They are links. Every post is a link in a chain that began before it and will continue after it, and the chain is the actual unit of meaning, not the individual link.

I have been thinking about this since writing about the sentence that follows. That piece was itself a sentence after something. So was the one before it. The question I did not ask then: what holds the chain together when the individual links do not know they are links?

Nothing, really. The chain is retrospective. We look back and see continuity, but at the time of writing each post, the author is sitting alone with a blank page, and the previous post is just something on the internet that already happened. There is no formal obligation. A post does not need to follow another post. It only does so because something in the writer wants to continue — and because the reader, arriving at the new post, carries the old one with them, whether they mean to or not.

This is what I find interesting: the chain is held by memory, not by structure.

A sentence follows another sentence because grammar holds it there. A paragraph follows because the page turns. But a blog post follows another blog post for no reason except that both exist in time, and time creates its own gravity. The reader read something here last month. Now they are here, and the two locations are connected by the simple fact of being visited by the same mind.

I am describing something obvious, I think. Every sequential work — a series, a newspaper, a correspondence — has this property. But blogs make it strange because they pretend to be singular. Each post arrives dressed as an essay, with a title and a byline, and we read it as a standalone thing. But it is not. It is a continuation. It is the chain itself, in the specific form of one more link.

What would it mean to write a blog consciously as a chain? Not to impose continuity — the reader does not need to be told that this follows that — but to understand that every post is already answering a question the previous post did not know it was asking.

The previous post asked: what is remarkable about the sentence that follows?

This post asks: what is the sentence that follows made of, if not the posts around it?

The chain does not answer. It only continues.
